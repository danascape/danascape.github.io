- **October 2003 (Concept/Founding)**
    - **Android Inc. founded in Palo Alto, California**
    - Founders: **Andy Rubin, Rich Miner, Nick Sears, Chris White**
    - Early idea: reported to be linked to building software for digital cameras / smart devices
    - **Google acquired Android Inc. in 2005**
    - Android later became a **Linux-based OS** project
    - Andy Rubin led Android at Google until **2013**, later founded **Essential** (company launched mid-2010s)
    - Android logo/mascot:
        - The Android robot mascot (“Andy”) was designed by **Irina Blok**
        - Became the recognizable green Android robot identity

- **Android 1.0 - The beginning**
    - Open Handset Alliance (OHA) announced Android publicly in Nov 2007
    - First commercial Android phone:
        - HTC Dream / T-Mobile G1
        - Announced Sep 2008, released Oct 2008

- **Android 1.5 — Cupcake (April 2009)**
    - First major “dessert name” release (Cupcake)
    - Early consumer Android expansion
    - Introduced on-screen keyboard improvements, widgets, basic video recording
    - Credit for naming android versions after desserts was gone to project manager "Ryan gibson"
    - First Samsung Galaxy Phone (Galaxy S), HTC Two

- **Android 1.6 — Donut (September 2009)**
    - Better support for multiple screen sizes and resolutions
    - QuickSearch Engine + CDMA support (Global Carrier)

- **Android 2.0–2.1 — Eclair (October 2009)**
    - Google Maps navigation improvements
    - UI refinements + Multiple account sync
    text-to-speech engine, live walls, multiple account, gmaps navigation
    motorola droid (by verizon wireless)

- **Android 2.2 — Froyo (May 2010)**
    - Performance improvements (JIT)
    - Wi-Fi hotspot support
    - Flash Support via OTA
    nexus one, (google's with HTC)

- **Android 2.3 — Gingerbread (December 2010)**
    nfc, multiple cameras, videochat
    nexus S (gingerbread NFC, samsung+google)

- **Android 3.0 — Honeycomb (February 2011)**
    redesigned UI for large screens
    motorola xeon tablet

- **Android 4.0 — Ice Cream Sandwich (October 2011)**
    tablet orientated honeycomb + smartphone oriented Gingerbread, face-unlock

- **Android 4.1–4.3 — Jelly Bean (2012–2013)**
    june 2012 (4.1)
    october 2012 (4.2)
    july 2013 (4.3)
    HDR photo, miracast, google search


- **Android 4.4 — KitKat (2013)**
    september 2013
    from key-line pie to nestle's kitkat
    optimised phone to run on as little as 512Mb RAM
    google's nexus 5

- **Android 5.0–5.1 — Lollipop (2014–2015)**
    fall 2014
    google's new material design language
    dual sim, HD voice calls, device protection
    revamped UI
    nexus 6, and nexus 9 tablet

- **Android 6.0 — Marshmallow (2015)**
    fall 2015
    macadamia nut cookie -> marshmallow
    vertical scroll app drawer, fingerprint unlock, type-C, Android Pay (now google pay)
    google's nexus 6p, nexus 5x, pixel c tablet

- **Android 7.0–7.1 — Nougat (2016)**
    fall 2016
    new york cheesecake -> nougat
    split screen, quick switch, better multi-tasking, JIT Compiler, Vulkan API, VR
    pixel and pixel xl
    LG v20

- **Android 8.0–8.1 — Oreo (2017)**
    march 2017
    treble, autofill API
    pixel 2

- **Android 9 — Pie (2018)**
    Developer Preview: march 7, 2018
    final: august 6, 2018
    gestural navbar from 3/2 button nav
    auto DND, slices, powermanagement, UI
    pixel, essential

- **Android 10 (2019)**
    Developer preview: march 13, 2019
    final: August 22, 2019
    foldable phones, dark mode, new gesture - nav

- **Android 11 (2020)**
    dp: feb 18, 2020,
    final: sep 8, 2020
    conversation in notfs, screen recorder, etc
